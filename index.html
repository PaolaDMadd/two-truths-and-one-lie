A little joking, a little vitalistic, the "differently young" Italian expression defines a person who has reached more or less the threshold of "advanced maturity", between fifty and sixty years. A person aware of having gained a lot in energy, spirit and life expectancy, compared to the counterparts even half a century ago.
A person that the job market bias would tend to "expel", in favour of a more flexible and malleable generation, because less expensive and more ready to master technologies such as the digital one. original version

When I decided to change career I received a lot of negatives comments that made me think I was too old for this change, and the 2015 movie The Intern came to my mind. There are a lot of conference and a huge effort to raise awareness in the tech industry and not only, where diversity in tech it's a common topic, but what is really diversity? Can it work in other industries too? I decided that I will start writing a blog about my journey, trying to give an answer to these questions. In the meantime you can enjoy the movie.

Paola Maddaluno

junior frontend developer

Sometimes people works for years before asking themselves if they are truly happy about their life and achievements. In the tourism industry, the high people turn-over and long working hours can lead to easily lose connection with ourselves, our real passions or our goals in life.

After almost 20 years, working in tourism and student's program exchange, I was missing myself and my creativity. So when the opportunity of a career change arose in October 2018, I took it!

I got my first job as a marketing assistant for an e-commerce. The first thing I had to learn was to set my thunderbird signature in HTML and CSS. This is also how my journey with coding started.

I signed up for the first workshop in town and started self-teaching me. I enjoy the creativity and the constant use of your brain to find a solution or learning something new.
During my journey, I discovered opportunities for learning to code in a safer environment where underrepresented groups, career changer and less young people are considered a plus, and every one is aimed by sharing the knowledge.

This made me confident, and because I am not afraid of changes or re-start my life in a new country; in June 2019, I decided to take a break from work and I invested all my time, energies and savings to to pursue my Life Project, became a web developer.
In the last year I spent my days building projects, writing/reading about coding, following workshops, lessons and conferences.

After being a student at codebar I joined this charity, first as an organizer and now as HTML, CSS coach. I love working with developers from industry and being involved in the community.

Unfortunately when I was ready for my job-hunting Covid-19 happened. This impacted to everyone, but I think for a career changer over 40y.o. made it tougher! To make this change happen, I understood that I needed a lift up, so I decided to try a bootcamp to improve my coding skills and learn backend languages. Also because I met a lot of career changer who thanking to bootcamps were able to create that bridge who led them to a new career path.
